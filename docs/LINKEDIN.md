⚽ **What if a stadium could think?**

For Hack2Skill PromptWars Challenge 4 (Smart Stadiums for FIFA World Cup 2026), I built **StadiumIQ** — and I set myself one rule: no "just a chatbot." GenAI had to make *decisions you can audit*.

What shipped, in one day of building:

🤖 **An agentic incident pipeline you can watch.** A fan faints in sector E1 → three AI agents hand off live on screen: Triage (severity + risk factors from real-time crowd density) → Resource (allocates actual steward/medic units with ETAs) → Comms (staff radio call + a panic-free PA announcement… or the deliberate decision to say nothing publicly).

🔮 **A what-if digital twin.** "What if we close Gate B at halftime?" → AI converts the question into simulation parameters, a deterministic crowd engine re-runs the matchday twice, and AI explains the verdict with mitigations. The model reasons; it never invents the numbers.

📢 **One-click emergency broadcast in 24 languages** — the languages of all 48 qualified nations, composed in a single AI call, with voice playback and proper RTL for Arabic.

🗣️ **A fan companion that speaks every fan's language** — literally. Type or *talk* in Spanish, Hindi, Swahili — it detects, answers in kind, and lights up your destination on a live stadium map. Easy-read and high-contrast modes built in, because 80,000 fans includes everyone.

🌱 Every personalized matchday plan ends with the greenest route home.

Stack: Next.js 16 · Google Gemini (JSON mode) · a seeded TypeScript crowd simulation · pure SVG visuals · zero database.

Biggest lesson: the strongest GenAI pattern isn't prompt → answer. It's **language model on the ends, deterministic engine in the middle** — AI translates human intent into parameters and computed results back into human judgment. That's what operations teams can actually trust.
